Dear Cloudflare Hiring Team,

I am applying for the Software Engineering Internship because Cloudflare feels like the kind of place
where engineers get to work on problems that are both technically hard and actually matter. I like
backend and infrastructure work most when it sits close to performance, reliability, and security,
and that is what pulled me toward this role.

I spent the last few weeks building cf ai research scout, a Cloudflare-native AI research assis-
tant. It uses AIChatAgent and Durable Objects for stateful WebSocket chat, WorkflowEntrypoint
for durable multi-step digest generation, D1 for storage, and Workers AI for embeddings and
streaming inference. Building it gave me a much clearer understanding of edge-native, stateful
architecture and how different it feels from traditional cloud backends. The products I would most
want to contribute to are Dynamic Workers and Workers AI—the idea of running AI-generated
code in lightweight isolates with fast cold starts feels like a really interesting systems problem.

Before starting my M.S. in Computer Science at UMass Amherst, I spent two years at Wysa as a
Backend Engineer building production systems for real clinical workflows. I worked on multi-tenant
NHS eTriage backends for UK clients, handling secure integrations and high-volume submissions.
I also owned backend security work—remediating vulnerabilities, hardening authentication, and
adding integration tests. That experience made me care a lot about building systems that keep
working under real constraints, not just ones that work once.

Outside of work, a lot of the projects I am most proud of are ones where I had to think like
a systems engineer. I built an inference service with async batching and caching to understand
throughput-latency tradeoffs, a distributed training simulator to study coordination overhead, and
a scalar-tensor autograd engine in C++ that pushed me to think carefully about execution graphs
and memory. I enjoy that kind of engineering: finding bottlenecks, cleaning up the design, making
the system behave better under load.

What makes Cloudflare interesting to me is that the software directly affects performance, relia-
bility, and security for a huge number of users on the Internet. I also like that Cloudflare values
people who build things instead of waiting for perfect instructions. That matches how I work best.
