Dear Cloudflare Hiring Team,

I am applying for the Software Engineering Internship because Cloudflare feels like the kind of place
where engineers get to work on problems that are both technically hard and actually matter. I like
backend and infrastructure work most when it sits close to performance, reliability, and security,
and that is what pulled me toward this role.

Before starting my M.S. in Computer Science at UMass Amherst, I spent two years at Wysa as a
Backend Engineer building production systems used in real clinical workflows. I worked on multi-
tenant NHS eTriage backends for UK clients, handling secure integrations, high-volume submissions,
and the kinds of reliability issues that are easy to ignore until they become painful in production. I
also owned backend security and hardening work, including remediating vulnerabilities, improving
authentication and rate limiting, and adding integration tests to protect against regressions. That
experience made me care a lot about building systems that do not just work once, but keep working
under real constraints.

Outside work, a lot of the projects I am most proud of are the ones where I had to think like a
systems engineer rather than just write application code. I built an inference service with async
batching and caching using Python, FastAPI, Redis, and Docker, mainly because I wanted to
understand how throughput, latency, and concurrency trade off against each other in practice. I
also built a distributed training simulator to study coordination overhead across workers, and a
scalar-tensor autograd engine in C++ that pushed me to think more carefully about execution
graphs, memory, and implementation details. I enjoy that kind of engineering a lot: understanding
where the bottlenecks are, cleaning up the design, and making the system behave better under
load.

What makes Cloudflare especially interesting to me is that the engineering work is not hidden
behind layers of abstraction from the real problem. The software directly affects performance,
reliability, and security for a huge number of users and developers on the Internet. That is the
kind of environment I want to learn in. I also like that Cloudflare seems to value people who are
proactive and build things instead of waiting for perfect instructions. That matches how I work
best.

I would be excited to contribute as an intern, learn from engineers working on Internet-scale systems,
and spend the summer shipping something useful.
