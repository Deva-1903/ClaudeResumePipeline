# Motivation & Voice Bank

> Reusable, **company-agnostic** narrative fragments for free-text application boxes ("Why are you interested?", "Anything else we should know?", "Why are you looking?"). These are the stable building blocks; the company-specific part (their language, domain, tech) gets re-derived from each JD, the same way `/lean-apply` assembles a resume from stable truth + per-JD framing.
>
> **Rules:**
> - Same truth hierarchy as everything else — every fragment here must be backed by `01_verified_claims.md` / `04_project_bank.md` / `05_bullet_bank.md` / the brain dump. Do not add aspirational or invented motivations.
> - These are *voice*, not resume bullets — first person, casual is fine. Em-dashes are OK here (unlike resume bullets).
> - **Never** store or reuse a "how did you hear about us" answer — it is always company/channel-specific and must be filled in fresh each time. See the note at the bottom.
> - Assemble an answer by picking 2–3 fragments that fit the role, then add one company-specific hook drawn from the JD. Do not paste all of them.

## How I think / engineering values

- [from-scratch] I build things from scratch to actually understand them — I wrote reverse-mode autodiff over matrix-valued operations by hand, and implemented RealNVP normalizing flows and DDPM diffusion models in JAX, before letting a framework do it for me.
- [whole-stack] I'd rather understand the whole stack than glue libraries together.
- [correctness] I care about getting the details right under load — profiling Spark stages in the Spark UI to track down shuffle and skew bottlenecks, and owning a backend where correctness genuinely mattered.
- [first-principles] I default to first principles: if I can't rebuild a piece of it myself, I don't trust that I understand it yet.

## Production ownership / stakes

- [ownership] Before grad school I owned a multi-tenant production backend (NHS clinical eTriage) with real UK clients and sensitive data, so I take code reviews, integration tests, and monitoring seriously — not as overhead, but as the job.
- [stakes] I like work where a small mistake actually costs something; that's motivating to me, not scary.

## How I learn

- [out-of-depth] I learn fastest when I'm a little out of my depth.
- [how-over-what] I care more about how to think through a problem than knowing the answer up front — I'm comfortable not knowing a language or tool yet and picking it up quickly.

## Why this kind of company (template hooks — fill the bracket from the JD)

- [culture-pull] What actually pulls me toward {company} is the engineering culture, not the {domain}.
- [people] I want to be in a room full of people who'd rather understand a system deeply than ship the quickest patch.

## Why I'm looking / where I am now

- [status] I'm a full-time MS CS student at UMass Amherst (graduating May 2027) looking for a summer/co-op internship where I can do real production work, not toy projects.
- [transition] I spent two years as a backend engineer before grad school; I came back to school to go deeper on systems, performance, and ML, and I'm looking for an internship that lets me apply that.

## "How did you hear about us?" — do NOT bank this

This is always specific to the actual channel (a friend, a talk, a course, a referral, a posting). Never reuse a fabricated source across applications. Leave it as a fill-in placeholder in any draft:

`[How I heard: <swap in the real source>]`
